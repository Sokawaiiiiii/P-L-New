Interpretation:

  • Gross Profit Margin: Indicates how much profit you make from each sale after accounting for the direct costs of producing your goods or services. A higher margin suggests efficient production.
  • Operating Profit Margin: Shows the profitability of your core business operations before interest and taxes. It reflects how well your business is managed from an operational standpoint.
  • Net Profit Margin: Represents the percentage of revenue left after all expenses, including COGS, operating expenses, interest, and taxes, have been deducted. This is your "bottom line" profit.

Analysis & Insights:

These insights are based on general industry benchmarks. Actual "good" or "bad" margins can vary significantly depending on your specific industry, business model, and growth stage. Always compare your figures to industry averages for a more accurate assessment.
